# The Creature — public-domain source art

Public-domain Frankenstein illustrations, grabbed (Hallie's go-ahead) as source material for The
Creature's art. The Creature is the disclosed-AI character, so found PD art is fair game here — these
are raw scans to crop/treat into card + character art later, not final assets.

All are **public domain** (pre-1929; artists long dead). Credit where it's nice to, not required.

| file | what it is | source |
|------|-----------|--------|
| `frankenstein_frontispiece_1831_vonholst.jpg` | The 1831 frontispiece — the Creature just animated, recoiling; Victor fleeing; a skull + an open **book** on the floor. THE canonical image. High-res (2816×3636). | Theodore von Holst, engraved by W. Chevalier, 1831. [Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Frontispiece_to_Frankenstein_1831.jpg) |

## Ideas for use (Hallie's call)
- The frontispiece figure → the Creature's **character art** (the body recoiling from its own making).
- The open book in the scene → motif for the Lesson/Book cards.
- The skull → Grief / Vexing Memory / the dead you stay with.
- 19th-c. engraving texture + 1818 typography → card frame / UI treatment for the whole character.
- Public-domain anatomical / automaton / botanical plates pair well for the (future) Parts roster.
